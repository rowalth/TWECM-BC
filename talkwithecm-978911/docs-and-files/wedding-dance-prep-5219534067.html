Wedding Dance Prep

Wedding dance

What are YOU looking for?
Here are the general elements:
  • something for the crowd -> showy / entertainment/ funny
  • Romance
  • Something for you -> sweet & close

What you don’t want - high school sway / talking / nervous / not for you and not for them

What my goal for you is - something you feel comfortable doing, something you’ll look back on and smile, something G’s mom will say ‘that was more meaningful than I expected’

Still choosing a song? see comments below.

OUR PLAN:

  1. Think about the dance space 
    1. Left/right/front 
  2. Get to know the music, pattern of a song… 
    1. Build up 
    2. Climax 
    3. Resolve 
    4. Chorus / verse / instrumental / solo / bridge 
  3. Think of ideas / strategies for each piece - I can provide ALL of this, but if you have anything BRING IT! 
    1. Start with high school sway…and DON’T TELL ANYONE THAT I’M HELPING YOU WITH THIS UNTIL AFTER 
    2. 2 turns he/she 
    3. Promenade 
    4. Bring in 

HOMEWORK:
Practice with a few steps / songs

Hi! For me, the answer depends on your goals & audience. For us, for example, we choose a mid-tempo song that was familiar, but not overdone. Lyrics are also important to me & played a key role in the final decision.


Rather than suggesting specific songs, I'll give you the steps I found useful for narrowing down & choosing our music.


1. Start collecting songs that you like, without reservation. Keep a running list.


2. Think about your goals. Do you want to spotlight your dancing skills, flex your choreography muscles, or simply enjoy a relaxing dance in the middle of all of the celebratory activities? Define & agree on goals together :)


3. Think about the audience. Do you want to entertain them? Please them? Impress them? Shock them? Or leave them totally out of the picture? If you wish to convey a specific message to your audience, song selection can really set the mood.


4. Go back to the running list of songs. Add a few more. Then with both your goals & audience in mind start narrowing down your choices.

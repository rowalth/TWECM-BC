INtroverts in business = It used to be really hard. I started by looking at the presentations that other people did and what made the difference between the ones that were really good and the ones that weren't. What I discovered was that the difference between a presenter that is meh or bad and one that's amazing is that amazing presenter don't care what other people think of them. They put on a performance they can enjoy for themselves and don't think about their audience in that way.
So I tried "acting" out my presentations vs. just giving them. Which worked well enough, but what if I wasn't in the mood to be social or present-y, or compelling?
I learned a few body hacks that helped over the years. One is to use super(wo)man poses or power poses before I had to be social. (I learned this from a Ted Talk). The other is to pump myself up, I use to do a cheer, by myself, jumping up and down like a crazy person clapping and saying, "to be enthusiastic, you must be enthusiastic". It looks dumb. It feels dumb. But damnit if it doesn't actually work.
The last thing I learned is that, oddly enough, it's not about me. If I'm meeting someone for the first time, I have a few questions in my backpocket to get them talking about themselves. This serves two purposes: 1. I don't have to talk, 2. They walk away with good feelings about me because I was interested in them.
It's also not about me in business. I'm always willing to go the extra mile for someone else other than myself. So when I think about a client or a potential client that is sitting around wasting hours or days at work when they don't have to, all because they didn't know about how to create systems in their business to free up their time and energy, then it makes it easier to reach out, post the thing, create a blog post (I just started posting some very real, very personal things on my blog recently) because if I'm helping one other person, then I'm going to do it.
Hope this helps

Hi Elizabeth,

Our discussions really triggered my engines and helped me clarify the "Romanian community" project. Many thanks :-)

As we talked, the main decision is to put this project on hold and rather focus on the "Romanian educator at home" idea, however this is a longer term project that doesn't require any immediate action.

I wrote below the answers to the questions from both our sessions.

Hope you are dong well! Have a nice weekend!
Diana


------------
1. I'm really glad that I created this group because now...

- our children are learning Romanian together, with a teacher, in an organized environment

- now I can control when, where and who is meeting, without sacrificing my flexibility

2. My takeaways from the first meeting - things that I heard and don't want to forget - from the first session are:

A. The approach to discuss the topic/project:
- what do you want to accomplish?
- what's holding you back?
- imagine what it is going to be in 3-6 months
- make a list of all the things that I could personally get out of this

B. The thought that participants to meetings could be kindly requested to make a donation. I just found this to be a crucial input regarding how MY time and effort is valuable, therefore should not be spent lightly.

3. Romanian community ideas:
- book reading meeting in the park
- book reading meeting at a library 
- meeting with families & children at an indoor playground (e.g. Dugu), to mainly encourage parents to socialise

4. My takeaways from the second meeting - things that I heard and don't want to forget - from the first session are: 

That sometimes one need the courage to honestly ask "why am I doing this? what am I getting out of this? is this really a worthy investment of my time and effort?" in order to get things moving in the right direction.

5. Don't do list



- don't organise meetings (don't even think about it!)

- don't spend time and effort to energise the whatsapp group (sending resources, asking how people are doing, etc.)
- don't spend time and effort to foster the contacts that until now have not shown engagement (i.e. work only with the people interested)
6. Ideas of resources for language learning:- online websites for babysitters (look for native speakers)- willhaben- word of mouth- Saturday schools- VFN and Women of Vienna families (look for teacher /educator)

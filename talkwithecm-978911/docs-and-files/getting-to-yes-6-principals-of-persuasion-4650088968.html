Getting to YES -> 6 principals of persuasion

Science Of Persuasion = SHORT CUTS + ETHICAL MANNER = YES!

1. Reciprocity. We are obliged to give if we have been given something/RECEIVE. (party, favor/whatever)
  • Experiment: Tips - Does the giving of a mint have any influence on the tip you leave?
  • 1 mint/fortune cookie +3%
  • 2 mint +14%
  • 1, pause, then 2nd mint +23%
  • Key principle: BE THE FIRST TO GIVE PERSONALIZED & UNEXPECTED
2. Scarcity. If it's scarce, we want it more. Use this principle by highlighting the Benefits, Unique and what you will LOSE if you don't do/have/consider this thing

3. Authority. We are more likely to comply with a request if it is coming from a perceived authority/expert. CREDIBLE / KNOWLEDGEABLE / EXPERTS -> Signal to others that you are credible/knowledgable/expert before making request - wearing a uniform, displaying medical certificates / diplomas /etc. 

(Introductions from others WORK - the science says even if the person is connected to you & will benefit, it doesn't change the fact that dropping some juicy info about you really will persuade people to trust you.

4. Consistency. We want to be consistent with our past commitments, even if the initial commitment is much smaller.
Looking for / asking for small commitments before the big ask. 
Experiment - simple sign in yard support a drive safely campaign
group a - very few people were willing....
group b -4x as many people 400% INCREASE - why, because 10 days before they had already committed to posting a small post card sized sticker in their window - 400% increase

Voluntary +active + public commitments = ideally in writing 


5. Liking. We like people who are similar, who give us compliments and who co-operate with us.
  • similar
  • compliments
  • cooperate with us on mutual goals
Ok, so what does this have to do with persuasion, business, getting to yes?? EVERYTHING 
Experiment: 
2 groups of MBA students entered into business negotiations
  • group a - 'time is money' get down to business 55% were able to reach a negotiated outcome
  • group b - before you begin negotiating, exchange some personal info, identify a similarity you share in common, then negotiate - 90% came to agreements typical worth about 20% more to both parties 

6. Consensus. If others (especially similar others) are doing it, then we are more likely to do it ourselves.
Hotel towels - 
eco 35% compliance
75% of guests reuse towels - 26% increase
75% of people who stay in THIS room reuse their towel - 33% increase

6 Scientifically validated principles of persuasion that provide for SMALL, PRACTICAL & often COSTLESS changes that can lead to BIG differences in your ability to influence and persuade others in an entirely ethical way.

1. Have you ever made an exception? Would it be possible to make one exception this time? -- gate keepers, trying to follow the rules, trigger their memory of making an exception

2. If I were in your position, I'd feel the same exact way...(give details to validate their standpoint), but X,Y,Z.... helps, break down dug in resistance.

7 psychological tricks of a making first impression

1. be enthusiastic 
  • smile, humor, engaging 
2. show respect FIRST
  • most people will respect/be polite/kind/helpful those who respect them first
  • be respectful to all of those around you, not just the one you're trying to impress...think of a date, respectful to me, but terrible to the waitress/dog
3. be open
  • don't lie, act enthusiastic about something you're actually not...
  • but be open about trying new things...
  • curious to have new experiences
common mistakes
1. showing up late
  • my time is more important than yours
  • sets a bad feeling to start
2. FILLER WORDS
  • um
  • uh
  • like
  • you know
  • slow down...there is nothing wrong with pausing, silence
3. avoid eye contact
  • confident, attentive, interested - stronger bond, eyes = window to the soul
4. focus on ME
  • talking about me
  • answering phone/ responding to messages during a conversation
  • forgetting their name
  • don't ask questions
1. set a playful tone from the start - 
  • walk into a room = high energy, joking, engaging, social momentum up, easier to connect with key players 
  • most people are nervous, quiet, small movements, don't notice me...which makes finding the right moment to stand up more tricky
  • I'M NOT SAYING YOU NEED TO BE A CLOWN...keep it real, true to you, match the setting/ context...but imagine how you want to feel at the end of the meeting/party/whatever, and enter with THAT tone.

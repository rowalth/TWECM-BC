Let's get started:

Step 1: Discover yourself - This stage is all about taking a serious look at who you are and what skills and talents you bring to the table.  Because you already have a lot to share with others and add value.
  • The first thing is to identify your professional self - dig deep & really discover who you are. What experiences have you already had that make you who you are today? What are your successes? What challenges did you face? What have you tried & failed? Considering both your successes & failures helps give a fuller picture of yourself. Because it is not so much about what happened that is important, but rather what have you learned from the experience and can apply to new situations that really matters. In other words, if you gained knowledge & learned from your failures, that is really valuable!
  • Be Memorable - How much do you know about Charisma & First impressions? We've all met that person, you know the one who seems to be completely comfortable and confident & it feels good just to be around them. Well, for most people these things don't just come naturally, they are learned & you can learn them too. Walking into a room, how do you decide who to approach? How do you start a conversation? How much eye contact is enough & how much is too much? Learning how to read subtle signals from others and Mastering the art of body language enables you to connect with others on a whole new level and make a lasting impression.
  • Conquer your inner critic - This is key. I'm talking about that little voice inside your head that says you can't do it, or worse that you shouldn't even try. Why would anyone listen to me? What if I don't have the right credentials? What if they think I'm stupid? Your inner critic can be a good guide to challenge yourself and to make good decisions, but it should not paralyse you or stop you from taking action. Remember that there are a lot of things about you that people can't even see until you show them:
    • Your education/experience
    • Your hard work to get where you are today
    • Your kind personality
(break)

Step 2: Build your message - In this step, it is time to focus on the tools and frameworks you need to find your own voice and create a compelling message. Zero in on the exact words you will use to deliver your message, as well as the essential components you need to captivate others, such as how to use
storytelling 
humor
a call to action
& staying true to your own personal style

  • First consider your Key statement - What is your main message? What is the most important thing that you want people to know after you walk away? Creating one power packed sentence that combines the most essential elements of your message makes it easier for you to build your whole message. What is the best way to construct your key message? How can you drive home the main idea, but is still be relatable without being too boring or forced? Well built key statements act like a filter system - making it clear what should be included in your message & (more importantly) what needs be left out.
  • Make your message irresistible - Whether you are having a quick chat at a happy hour or preparing a 4 hour workshop, what will you do to captivate your audience, keep things things interesting and have asking for more?
  •  Do you want to start with a question? Tell a joke? 
  • Across the globe and across cultures, people respond to stories. There is some magic about a good story that draws us in & makes us want to know more. What happens next? Will the hero win? The more relatable the story is, the more effectively you can keep your listeners tuned in.
  • In my blueprint, I guide you to use the power to story telling create your message and captivate your audience.
  • Consider when it is appropriate to add personal information & how much are you comfortable sharing.
  • No fail messaging! -This is so important. I wish someone had told me about this before. Once you have narrowed in on your key statement & built up your message to make it irresistible, it's finished, right?  Only if you want to be average. If you want be outstanding, now is the time to add the final touches to polish & get your message ready to deliver.  Consider different settings and applications. How can you adjust your 45 min presentation, for a quick blurb or promotional text? Or how can you make your sales pitch without being salesy? To finish, don't forget to add a compelling call to action with clear plans to follow up.

  • First it is important to know your audience, your true audience. At this point in your life, you have probably already realised that EVERYONE has an opinion. It is important to be able to filter this information and know what is valuable for you and what is just noise. Who are the 5 key people you want to know? Who has the knowledge/ experience/ connections/ what ever it is that you need. Be specific - Get names & contact details. Dig deeper & research the exact people who can give you what you want. What are their pains? How can you add value for them? Get to know their needs better than they know themselves.
  • Respond to the unknown. You've heard the horror stories & if you are anything like me you've lived through a few! What do you do when the power goes out? The microphone doesn't work, or your slides were deleted? I've lived through it all! Now I won't say that every experience was a huge success, but I can say that I've learned a LOT and I want to pass it on to you -  in A Survival kit for when things are out of control.

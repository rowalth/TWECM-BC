Now that you have a clearer idea about how to set yourself up for a great introduction, now it is time to put all of this into practice. Or more specifically, how to answer that dreaded question, "So, tell me, what do you do?"



My Super Savvy Introduction 
I - solo work - WORKSHEET

1) I'm a _______________ (what you do /job title or general description)
work with some ideas here - deli-lama!

  1. Master of Disaster - helps federal, state, and local authorities access the information they need to recover quickly from calamities - MapInfo Corp.
  2. President and TeaEO - play on words here for the CEO - Honest Tea
special note - in between jobs / career shift! add here

2) and I work with____________________________(who you support /boss, customers) 
this may seem like a simple question, but take some time to consider ALL of the people that you interact with and how you support them / make their lives better


3) helping them_________________________________(the big picture of what you do) 

4) so that they can __________________________(the results you make possible) 
this is extremely important - not only do you want to tell what you do, but this makes it crystal clear to your listener WHY your work is important. Ex - I help people lose weight...meh, I help people finally lose the weight they've been carrying around for years so that they can look and feel their best! - do you hear the difference? look and feel their best - I want that! Who doesn't want that? Right?

GOAL: Try to keep it authentic and conversational – get rid of anything too salesy or that just doesn’t feel like you.

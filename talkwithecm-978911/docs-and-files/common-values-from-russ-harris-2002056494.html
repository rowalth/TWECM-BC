Common Values from Russ Harris


Values are your heart’s deepest desires for how you want to behave as a human being. Values are not about what you want to get or achieve; they are about how you want to behave or act on an ongoing basis. 

There are literally hundreds of different values, but below you’ll find a list of the most common ones. Probably, not all of them will be relevant to you. Keep in mind there are no such things as ‘right values’ or ‘wrong values’. It’s a bit like our taste in pizzas. If you prefer ham and pineapple but I prefer salami and olives, that doesn’t mean that my taste in pizzas is right and yours is wrong. It just means we have different tastes. And similarly, we may have different values.

To help get a clear idea of your driving values read through the list below and write a letter next to each value: V = Very important, Q = Quite important, and N = Not so important; and make sure to score at least ten of them as V = Very important. 




  1. Conformity: to be respectful and obedient of rules and obligations 


  2. Cooperation: to be cooperative and collaborative with others 


  3. Courage: to be courageous or brave; to persist in the face of fear, threat, or difficulty 


  4. Creativity: to be creative or innovative 


  5. Curiosity: to be curious, open-minded and interested; to explore and discover 


  6. Encouragement: to encourage and reward behaviour that I value in myself or others 


  7. Equality: to treat others as equal to myself, and vice-versa 


  8. Excitement: to seek, create and engage in activities that are exciting, stimulating or thrilling 


  9. Fairness: to be fair to myself or others 


  10. Fitness: to maintain or improve my fitness; to look after my physical and mental health and wellbeing 


  11. Flexibility: to adjust and adapt readily to changing circumstances 


  12. Freedom: to live freely; to choose how I live and behave, or help others do likewise 


  13. Friendliness: to be friendly, companionable, or agreeable towards others 


  14. Forgiveness: to be forgiving towards myself or others 


  15. Fun: to be fun-loving; to seek, create, and engage in fun-filled activities 


  16. Generosity: to be generous, sharing and giving, to myself or others 


  17. Gratitude: to be grateful for and appreciative of the positive aspects of myself, others and life 


  18. Honesty: to be honest, truthful, and sincere with myself and others 


  19. Humour: to see and appreciate the humorous side of life 


  20. Humility: to be humble or modest; to let my achievements speak for themselves 
  21. Industry: to be industrious, hard-working, dedicated 


  22. Independence: to be self-supportive, and choose my own way of doing things 


  23. Intimacy: to open up, reveal, and share myself -- emotionally or physically – in my close personal relationships 


  24. Justice: to uphold justice and fairness 


  25. Kindness: to be kind, compassionate, considerate, nurturing or caring towards myself or others 


  26. Love: to act lovingly or affectionately towards myself or others 


  27. Mindfulness: to be conscious of, open to, and curious about my here-and-now 
    experience 


  28. Order: to be orderly and organized 


  29. Open-mindedness: to think things through, see things from other’s points of view, and weigh evidence fairly. 


  30. Patience: to wait calmly for what I want 


  31. Persistence: to continue resolutely, despite problems or difficulties. 


  32. Pleasure: to create and give pleasure to myself or others 


  33. Power: to strongly influence or wield authority over others, e.g. taking charge, 
    leading, organizing 


  34. Reciprocity: to build relationships in which there is a fair balance of giving and taking 


  35. Respect: to be respectful towards myself or others; to be polite, considerate and show positive regard 


  36. Responsibility: to be responsible and accountable for my actions 


  37. Romance: to be romantic; to display and express love or strong affection 


  38. Safety: to secure, protect, or ensure safety of myself or others 


  39. Self-awareness: to be aware of my own thoughts, feelings and actions 


  40. Self-care: to look after my health and wellbeing, and get my needs met 


  41. Self-development: to keep growing, advancing or improving in knowledge, skills, character, or life experience. 


  42. Self-control: to act in accordance with my own ideals 


  43. Sensuality: to create, explore and enjoy experiences that stimulate the five senses 


  44. Sexuality: to explore or express my sexuality 


  45. Spirituality: to connect with things bigger than myself 


  46. Skilfulness: to continually practice and improve my skills, and apply myself fully when using them 


  47. Supportiveness: to be supportive, helpful, encouraging, and available to myself or others 


  48. Trust: to be trustworthy; to be loyal, faithful, sincere, and reliable

Once you’ve marked each value as V, Q, N (Very, Quite, or Not so important), go through all the Vs, and select out the top six that are most important to you. Mark each one with a 5, to show it’s in your top five. Finally, write those five values out below, to remind yourself this is what you want to stand for as a human being.

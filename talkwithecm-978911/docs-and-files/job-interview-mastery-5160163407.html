Interview skills
google search brings up all kinds of advice...but what really annoys me, what gets my goat & gets me all steamed up & the nonsense people are writing about the 'soft questions.' You know what I mean, those seemingly innocent personal questions - tell me about yourself...What would you say is your greatest strength/weakness...what's more important: Being on time or being perfect? ARRGGG! right?
1.Let's unpack this -
sticky
seem totally innocent, no complicated words or deeply technical knowledge required...
so it seems easy to answer, like talking to a friend...chatty, chatty
BUT, it doesn't always flow so easy, monologueing like that, wrapping it up into a succinct package, ugg
- a friend once told me that  - it just feels like a trap!
2.
& a google search doesn't offer much more help -
Tips: be yourself, act natural...BUT IT'S AN INTERVIEW!
free flow is tough
what's the right answer? = there is no right answer...UGG, so what are they judging, how candidates sweat under pressure???
3.
There is 1 piece of advice that I totally agree with - PRACTICE!
What skill or task can you expect to do perfectly the first time?
PRACTICE - PRACTICE - PRACTICE

4.which is exactly what I've done!
I learned a lot, tested & tweaked my theories...& developed a framework to confidently answer even the slipperest of questions!
In my experience preparing in advance & knowing exactly where to start & how to structure answers is key, that way it's easy to simply plug in the details & delivery a polished answer every time.

WEEK 3

M - Job interview mastery
T - sarah joy testimonial - So I scored the interview. Then I panicked.


Given my work history, I've always had this niggling thought that maybe I'm just lazy and uninterested in anything as soon as it becomes "work". I had a rather negative story about myself as an employee, and this attitude wasn't going to help me in the interview. One of my dance friends, however, is a coach for exactly this kind of thing - she'd been gently bugging me to let her help for a while, when I'd not really been mentally in the right place for it - and now, now was the time! Lucky for me, she was (or made herself) able to fit me in last-minute.


Talking to Elizabeth was wonderful. It is also her job, so when we went past just the initial chat (which was already helpful), I did pay her (fair's fair!). I realise it's a privilege to be able to do so.


She took my story about myself and showed me the positive side of it, and helped me reformulate it into something I could speak about joyfully, instead of regretfully. She taught me how to introduce myself in a way that was far more engaging than my default ('Boring!') go-to history-of-self. We talked about the standard HR-style questions I dislike so much, and she helped me prepare answers so that I wouldn't choke or get stuck somewhere negative. It was exactly what I needed and I am SO thankful for her help.

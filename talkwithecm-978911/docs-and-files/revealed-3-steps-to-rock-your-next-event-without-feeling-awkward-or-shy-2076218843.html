Revealed: 3 steps to rock your next event without feeling awkward or shy

"Ugg! I wish that I had talked to you about this last week!" my friend moaned. We were catching up at a local coffee shop and she was feeling really frustrated. She had the opportunity to represent her company to a high profile client, and not just any client, but her dream employer - the exact company where she would love to work. 

"Awesome!" I cheered, but she didn't look happy about it at all. 

"No, it was awful." She felt blocked. She wasn't sure how to show that she was interested in joining their team without seeming too opportunistic or disloyal to her current job. So, in the end, she said nothing at all!

"It's like I was given the golden opportunity - my dream job! - and I just threw it away...ugg! But now I know exactly what to do and focus on for our next meeting!"

Whether you are looking for a job, want to grow your business or are looking to push your career to the next level, you know that networking is vital for your career. But, like my friend, you may still be unclear or even hesitant about what it really means to network. Whether your event is tonight, next week or next month, use this quick guide to zero in on the essential elements of networking to get the results you want. 

It seems that everyone knows that networking is important, but very few people actually know what is involved, what to do, or how to enjoy the process! When working with people to prepare for a networking event, I hear a lot of questions, like this:
  • Who should I talk to? 
  • What should I say? 
  • I hate small talk!
  • Is it even worth it?
The truth is that networking is about personal connections and creating relationships, not just getting a job or closing a sale. And no matter how advanced technology may be, face to face communications and personal connections still carry more weight and open more doors than emails and texts. Plus, events can be a super efficient way to meet a large number of like minded people in a short amount of time.
 
With just a bit of prework, you can come home from your next event with something much more valuable than a pile of random business cards and memories of cheese cubes. Read these tips so you’ll know exactly what to do before, during, and after your meeting to connect authentically with others and build up your power network.
 
Before:
  1. Dress the part. Appearances count, so make sure that you look good. Did you know that first impressions are already being formed before you even say a single word? If you’re not sure what to wear, check the website and promotional materials to get an idea of what’s appropriate.
  2. Rehearse. Practice introducing yourself, your 30 second pitch and some relevant small talk. You need to be prepared to speak and present yourself in a confident and interesting way. It also helps to have a few subjects in your pocket that you can discuss to create connections and build rapport.
  3. Volunteer & get involved. Helping out at events can be a great way to reduce your expenses and become engaged even if you tend to feel shy. Contact the organizers to find out about volunteer opportunities. 
Because I tend to be shy, I always try to volunteer for the sign-in desk. That way I can be the first smile they see when they arrive and, because we've already talked, I find it so much easier to start a conversation later too. -Janette K. 
 
During:
  1. Give value. Effective networking is based on being generous. Potential contacts are more likely to welcome your interest if you’re focused on what you can do for them rather than coming off as an opportunist who is only looking out for yourself.
  2. Make introductions. If you already know some of the attendees, think about who they might like to meet. Acting as a hub and providing introductions strengthens your network and helps others feel more comfortable as well. Being helpful and generous also makes it likely you’ll receive similar favors. You can also introduce people you just met to help keep conversations flowing.
  3. Refine your story. Networking events are a great time to focus on building relationships rather than selling. Use this opportunity to practice and test out your pitch in different ways. By not focusing on a sale, you can test introductions, conversation topics or even your elevator pitch with much less at stake. You’ll also have a wide audience to work with.
 
After:
  1. See it through. End Strong. Any event can be productive, but it is up to you to take strategic action with the contacts you just made. That might mean sharing a contact, sending an article or even inviting them out for coffee.
  2. Act quickly. Reach out as soon as possible so your last conversation will be fresh in their mind. You’ll make a positive impression and their interest may still be high.
  3. Review & reflect. Think of developing your networking skills as an ongoing project. After each event, reflect on what went well and what you can do differently next time.
 
Going to events and trying to network may seem like a lot of effort, but when you are warmed up and ready to go, the opportunities and potential for growth are absolutely worth it. Connecting with others is essential to build your power network, advance your career, and grow your business.

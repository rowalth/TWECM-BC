Getting out there and building up a POWER Network Challenge

September Challenge 
This is currently posted in my fb group. I'd like to transfer the core content to our online space as a free challenge month.
Let's do this IN facebook land - can we do a private group? (for challenge #2 mainly)

#1
Welcome to our September Challenge - Getting out there and building a POWER Network!


Imagine walking into a group of people with complete confidence because you know exactly what to say and do to add real value and have people wanting more. Or think of having connections with the right people in the right places to finally make things happen - That's what this month is all about!


Let's start by getting to know each other!

+Challenge: Write a post telling us a bit about you and why you are here. What do you want from your network? Additionally, tell us what is driving you? Take a moment and write out your 5 top values.
+Tip: Use this list of the most common values to help get you started - https://public.3.basecamp.com/p/nUuKEENNdXs3q1VouLqfYBxc
+Why: Knowing your values is crucial in giving you direction, inspiration and fulfilment.
+Want more? Learn how one woman landed the job of her dreams with a super 30 second pitch! https://talkwithecm.com/how-30-seconds-can-change-your-life-part-i/ 

#2
Our September Challenge - Getting out there and building your POWER Network. 
Challenge #2 - Let's talk about introductions!

+Challenge: Create a short video and introduce yourself.
+Tip: There is no need to prepare in advance or edit the video, be spontaneous and improvise on the spot. This will help us get to know each other better and spark connections with new people.
+Why: This gives us the chance to get to know each other better
+Want more? https://talkwithecm.com/introduce-yourself-without-feeling-awkward-or-fake/

#3
September Challenge - Getting out there and building a POWER Network!


Now it is time for challenge #3 = KINDNESS

This week is all about preforming random acts of kindness in our every day life.
+Challenge: Perform a random act of kindness, and share your experience with us!
+Tip: An act of kindness doesn’t have to be big. A simple compliment, paying for someone’s coffee, or just asking a cashier about their day is good enough. Here is a list of 103 additional ideas - https://www.bradaronson.com/acts-of-kindness/
+Why: A random act of kindness is a shortcut to becoming more positive, and making other people respond better to you.
+Want more? Join Start Your Conversation With Success and learn how to add real value in any situation.

#4
September Challenge - Getting out there & building up a POWER Network!


Challenge #4 - Your existing network! Rather than only pushing forward and focusing on people you don't know, remember to take the time and appreciate the relationships and network connections that you already have! We all know people, and it is a great idea to start building your network through people you already know and trust. Let's get started!

+Challenge: Think of at least 5 people that you already know. They can be people you feel close to, or people you are only slightly connected with and don't know that well yet. Choose one and write down five qualities you appreciate or admire about them let them know.
+Tip: Don’t text or call them - meet them in person! Stop by their desk at work, or meet for coffee.
+Why: Your social relationships need to be nurtured and cared for. By showing your appreciation you will strengthen already existing relationships.
+Want more? Here are 7 super tips to show appreciation at work, without being tacky - https://www.bustle.com/articles/131175-how-to-show-gratitude-at-work-without-looking-cheesy-in-7-different-ways

#5
September Challenge - Getting out there and building a POWER Network!


Challenge #5 - Reaching out


Now it is time to put everything together and start really expanding your power network.

+Challenge: Make a list of 10 people that you would like to get to know better, and then invite them out for coffee.
+Tip: Keep it simple, like this: “Hi, I saw your recent post on LinkedIn and I'd like to know more about that. Want to catch up over some coffee? My treat.”
+Why: If we want to build up connections and use our network to advance our career and social life, we need to learn to take the initiative.

1. 15 minute session structure


Job Interview Mastery Session
  • 1-3 minutes - introduction
    • Tell me who you are and what position or job you wish to apply for
  • 5 minutes - Be prepared to answer one or two of these questions
    • Tell me about yourself
    • Recall a time when you were assigned a task outside of your job description. How did you handle the situation? What was the outcome? 
    • What are the three things that are most important to you in a job?
    • Give an example of when you had to work with someone who was difficult to get along with. How did you handle interactions with that person?
    • Have you ever had to “sell” an idea to your coworkers or group? How did you do it? What were the results?
    • When was the last time you asked for direct feedback from a superior? Why? 
    • How do you determine what amount of time is reasonable for a task?
    • Is it better to submit a project that’s perfect and late, or one that’s good and on time? 
    • Surprise question!
  • 1-3 minutes - closing the interview 
    • What questions would you want to ask?
    • How would you finish the interview?
  • 5 minutes - Feedback & next steps
    • They want to know - Are you capable (skills/experience)? Are you a good match (personality/ethics/availability)?
    • You want to know - What can I offer and how can I be successful here? 


Building a Power Network Session
  • 1-3 minutes - introduction
    • Tell me who you are and your 30 second pitch (including what you want to find - job, business partners, etc.)
  • 5 minutes
    • We’ll create a blueprint - the specific who /what /when /where / how/ why to build up your personal power network
  • 1-3 minutes
    • Using a fill-in-the-blank scripts we’ll practice requesting and setting up informational interviews, meetings, coffee, phone interviews, etc.
  • 5 minutes - Feedback & next steps

3. Description
show off your best self and launch your career

You’re looking for a job and they are looking for an employee - are you a match?

Some people are just better at networking and interviewing than others. But that doesn’t necessarily mean they’re the right fit for the job. In this 15 minute session, you’ll be asked some difficult questions and challenged to present yourself well with little to no preparation, followed by a quick, but honest, tear-down session where you’ll get clear feedback about what works, what doesn’t and clear steps about exactly what you need to do next to show off your best self and launch your career.

Job Interview Questions:
  • Tell me about yourself
  • Recall a time when you were assigned a task outside of your job description. How did you handle the situation? What was the outcome? 
  • What are the three things that are most important to you in a job?
  • Give an example of when you had to work with someone who was difficult to get along with. How did you handle interactions with that person?
  • Have you ever had to “sell” an idea to your coworkers or group? How did you do it? What were the results?
  • When was the last time you asked for direct feedback from a superior? Why? 
  • How do you determine what amount of time is reasonable for a task?
  • Is it better to submit a project that’s perfect and late, or one that’s good and on time?
Great Questions to close the interview:
  • Culture
    • What does a typical week look like?
    • What do you expect from team members in this position?
    • What is the culture of the department? 
  • Success
    • What would you like to see someone in this position accomplish in the first 90 days?
    • How have past employees been successful in this position?
  • Future 
    • What are some of the challenges you foresee in the next 6 months that someone in this position will be working on?
    • Where do you see the company in five years?
  • KILLER QUESTION: Based on our conversation, is there anything leaving you with hesitancy for hiring me for this position? - Cass Thompson
    • Shows you can take feedback
      Gives opportunity to fight for it, 
      You’ll know where you stand & checking for open questions at the end

email scripts!

Here's a list of sample email scripts to help get you started. Use these as launch pads to create your own winning emails.
Copy the information in the emails and edit to your specific situation. I recommend that you add your own personalized touches so that the email sounds both friendly and professional.

The most important thing here is to focus on making the other person feel good and make it easy for them to say yes. No one likes to feel awkward or pressured to help someone they don't even know.


WARM EMAILS -
Writing to people you already know, or at least have had some contact with.


Email:​ Request an introduction from someone you know

Hi (name),
I hope you’re having a great week! 

Last week I drove by our old school and it made me think of you. It’s been awhile since we’ve caught up, let’s plan a coffee date soon!

Your recent post on LinkedIn really sparked my attention. It is so exciting to see how companies and schools are working together to welcome more women into the tech industry.  

Additonally, I wanted to let you know that I’ve been thinking about making a move to the (name your industry) and I noticed that you have some great connections. Would you be comfortable introducing me to (desired contact) at (company)? I’d love to talk to her about her role in the company and her work with (project name). If you wish, I’m happy to write up and send an introduction email to you so that you can simply copy and paste it.

Thank you in advance for your consideration! 
Also, please let me know when you’re available for that coffee date. I’m looking forward to catching up! 


Best, (name) 

How to ask for recommendations for people to talk to

Hello (name),

Hope all is well.

If you recall, we spoke a few months ago at (event) when I was exploring new career opportunities in (industry). Thanks again for (how they helped you).

I was browsing the (company) site the other day and the (job) caught my eye. I think it’d be perfect for me considering my work experience with (example).

From what I remember, it sounds pretty similar to the work you do at (company). By any chance, do you know of anyone there that you think I should talk with? I’d love to learn more about the role so I can see if it’s the right fit for me.

If not, no problem — just wanted to keep you in the loop. Thanks again for all your help!

Take care,
(name)


Follow up email:
I think sending a short email letting them know that you appreciate their contact/help/advice/whatever they gave you & that you sent the application as suggested is a good idea. People generally feel good knowing that someone actually listened & followed their suggestions :)

Something like this: 
Hello (name),
Just wanted to thank you again for talking with me at [name of event]. I sent my CV to (name) as you recommended. I’ll keep you in the loop, and of course, please let me know if there’s anything I can do to repay the favor!
Best,
(name)

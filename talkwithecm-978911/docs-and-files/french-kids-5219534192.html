Hello students,

How are you doing? What is the current situation where you are?


It’s been a strange week here, as things are opening back up in Vienna. Schools, restaurants & more.

Over the weekend, I went to my local Chinese restaurant and have a great time reconnecting after the long break. I have to admit, it is super fun to order food in Chinese! 

Speaking another language is a great skill to have, especially when it helps to make connections and open conversations that might not otherwise happen. 

Although it is easy for me to speak Mandarine now, it wasn’t always that way. After I left the restaurant, I thought about how hard it was for me to learn Chinese and build up my speaking skills. I remember one summer in particular when I thought that I would just have to give everything up. 

I had just finished the first year of a two year program. As students, we were all overwhelmed with the sheer amount of work and stress load of the program. Everyone was exhausted. Most of my classmates retreated at home for the summer, but I didn’t have that luxury. Because, besides having the full load of being student like the rest of the class, I was living an expat life and juggling a job, an internship and I had to do everything in Chinese! My goals were clear, but stress levels were high and time was limited. Yeah, I was totally ready to throw in the towel & quit. 
 
 But, I didn’t. 

I’m not going to say that it was easy, but over time I dedicated myself and tested different ideas & techniques to help me through. At first I searched online for tips & tricks..I found a few good ones, but most of them just turned out to be a big waste of time. Over time I developed foolproof ways to master language learning with micro-steps and mini-challenges that really work. Within a year I managed to not only get though, but I even had the highest marks in a few of our classes! 

I have packaged up everything I learned into a system that really works - like a language bootcamp - and I want to offer that to you.

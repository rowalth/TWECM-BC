Discover Yourself

FOUNDATION - Me & the value I add

1. Identify your professional self (Pre-work)

  • Creating  Confidence - Self confidence with a focus on the value you can add for others
    • Focus on VALUE - Focus on feeling confident about the value you are adding in any given situation. Everyone can do it (restaurant tip/knowing where the bathroom is, etc) this is where to hang your confidence on
  • Discovery worksheets - Your past accomplishments 
    • A. 15 y/n ?s - short answer, triggering your stories (business skills sets)
    • B. setting a framework (self), details of each memory (knowhow / behaviour / personal attribute)
    • C. examples

2. Be Memorable (IN class)
Charisma & First impressions/Mastering the art of body language

  • Be PROACTIVE
    • Take a few minutes to prepare yourself BEFORE you enter the room
      • Ask yourself - What value can I offer in this situation?
      • Develop a strong 10 second introduction
      • Prepare yourself with a few topics
        • Something in the news / tending on social media
        • Something specific to this group of people
        • Avoid controversial topics
      • Set your intention and stay focused on reaching your goal
      • The art of small talk 
        • Use the secret messages behind outward body cues to start conversations
        • Sample scripts with the exact words you can use to start a conversation
        • Take an active role to help the conversation flow in the direction you want
        • Keep the conversation exciting 
        • Use a clean forward thinking exit
        • Follow up as promised
      • Frameworks for approaching people
        • Ask yourself: How do I want this person/people to feel when talking to me? entertained/valued/attracted
        • 60 second challenge - challenge yourself to introduce yourself to someone (anyone) within 60 seconds of walking into a room.
        • Positive Feedback challenge - challenge yourself to give someone a genuine compliment (something they are wearing, or something they did).
  • Control what people think when they first meet you
    • Watch their face:
      • Be aware of engagement cues - Is the person really listening or simply waiting for another chance to speak?
        • manage engagement over time
        • Be aware of micro-expressions use them to your advantage -  Watch for OH moments!
        • Look for cues to deepen engagement & build connection
        • TOOL: chart (engagement over time)
    • Branching off opportunities that help you expertly shift the conversation flow
    • How to speak/present:
      • Use pauses & silence effectively to sound more professional, drink water! when presenting
      • Use sample scripts to start, shape & end conversations
    • How charismatic people / great leaders communicate - watch learn from the people who command your attention!
      • Effectively use body language & personal space to deliver your deeper message
        • move only with intention & purpose (imagine of the man with the cup. confident / clear, not nervously checking his watch...)
      • Smiling - This is a great way to connect with others, but over doing it can seem fake or even desperate.
      • Nodding - This is a great way to show engagement, but over doing has the same risk as smiling too much. 
      • Eye Contact - Eyes are like a window into the soul of a person. What are your listeners eyes telling you? eye contact challenge!
        • Utilise proven eye contact techniques to help build trust and create intimacy 

3. Conquer your inner critic (after class)
  • Address your concerns: Why would anyone listen to me? What if I don't have the right credentials? I don't have anything new to offer.
  • Remember that people can't see these things until you show them:
    • Your education/experience
    • Your hard work to get where you are today
    • Your kind/funny personality

  • 6. No fail messaging! (IN class)

      • Real world applications

Engage your Audience

 - applying the craft
Adjust your message to multiple situations

7. Know your true audience (pre-work)
  • Who are the 5 key people you want to know? 
    • Be specific - names, contact details
    • Research the exact people who can give you what you want
    • What are their pains/motivations?
    • Get to know their needs better than they know themselves
  • Who is the audience - who is my target audience? 
    • What are their pains/motivations?
    • Favorite Topics = Me, Myself & I 
    • People don't really care about you :( Think of ways to reach THIS person.
8. Respond to the unknown (IN class)
  • Horror stories
    • The Ultimate Awkward Situations
      • tech problems - lights go out
      • awkward situations
      • challenges from audience
  • Practical Applications
    • How to answer difficult questions in an interview
    • How to manage tech problems - What to do when the lights go out
    • How to change challenges from audience into wins - secrets from the PR world
    • How to answer tricky questions in an interview
  • Survival kit for when things are out of control (develop IN class)
    • Step back & remove yourself from the situation (at least mentally)
    • Don't seek to blame, or overanalyse the situation - these behaviours tend to bring the negative aspects of the situation into focus rather than solutions
    • Use helpful language and questions to assess the current situation and resources available
    • Remember a calm mind is often your most valuable resource for making good decisions!
9. Be sensational! (practice IN class)
  • Consider multiple settings: happy hour, small talk, networking, pitch, interview, presentation, medium (live/ppt/digital)
  • Some people are wonderful presenters...but fall apart when it comes to small talk, or others can be the lift of the party, but stammer and stumble when it comes to giving a presentation. Sensational people have learned to how to adjust their message and master matching multiple contexts and situations. I guess that you already know where your own strengths are, now let's take a look at ways to transfer your strengths to other settings.
  •  
  • tone - formality, are we in a business context - where they expect me to deliver a pitch, or are we at a ropes course team building event, am I at an after work happy hour or sitting in a job interview?
    • match your language to the setting - formal, casual, slag / jokes vs. serious
    • interaction? Conversation vs. lecture, even in a lecture, I encourage you to Interact with your audience - This helps make the presentation more engaging, holds the audience‚Äôs attention, and increases the amount of information they take away from your talk.
      • This could mean Including pauses in your presentation, 
      • asking and encouraging people to ask for questions, if they need clarification on any of the presentation. 
      • You can also address people in the audience directly, either by name or by simply looking them in the eye. 
    • # people - 1:1 - small group - large group , taking a moment to think about the shifting dynamics in advance helps you to ease into the transition
    • Open vs. closed bubble , TV interview, panel discussion, small group, think about body language as we discussed in Module 1.
    • A few tips about how to be the centre of conversation & how to pass the spotlight to someone else - eye contact & body language again play a huge roll here. Note where your body language says that your attention is going, and what about others around you? with a question / making a comment - pass question / introduction
  • time - Think about adjusting your message to different time allotments
    • This is an easy one to prepare and practice in advance
    • the only thing you need is a timer :)
    • Time adjustments (10-30 seconds, 5 - 40 min)
      • 10 second introduction
      • 30 pitch
      • 5 minute interview, a 40 min presentation or a 5 day workshop
    • The 30 seconds that could change your life
  • tools - there is not ONE answer that is right for every situation, but taking a few moments to think ahead will ensure that you feel confidence with the right tools when it really matters. 
    • Communication aids? business cards/ppt/CV
    • medium (live/ppt/digital)
    • Which communication tools & when? 
      • PPT - 
        • Put together a simple slideshow. Use the slideshow as a way to emphasize key points, not to explain them in detail. Otherwise you risk that your audience will just look at the slides instead of paying attention to what you are saying.
          • Use only a small amount of text or visual material per slide. For instance, if you are outlining three goals for your organization‚Äôs upcoming year, make a slide that says ‚ÄúGoal 1: Boost membership 10%.‚Äù
          • That is enough information to remind the audience of the point you want to make, but not so much that they will stop paying attention.
      • printed materials - business cards, brochures, networking outreach plan CV, etc.

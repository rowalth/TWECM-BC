Hidden Job Market

Quiz:
What is the hidden job market?
It includes:
-Jobs that are announced publicly, but not promoted widely
-Jobs that organisations hide from the public & filled by referrals only
-Jobs that are never publicised anywhere
-All of the above


(answer is all of the above)

What kinds of jobs are there?
Why Would a Job Be Hidden?
Ultimately, employers want to hire people they know, like, and trust. Receiving applications, resumes, and conducting interviews are great ways for employers to get to know job candidates, but it’s also a time-consuming process. For an employer, the search for a potential employee begins well before they post a job. It might include figuring out what skills and experience are necessary for the job, writing a job description, and setting salary and benefit options with their finance and human resource staff.

There may be good reasons why an employer doesn’t post or advertise a job. Employers will leverage networks for a number of reasons.  They oftentimes have an immediate need to fill a position, and the cycle time to find a candidate through an online posting is relatively long. Consider these scenarios:

  • An employer needs to find a replacement for a sales representative who works on many client or business accounts. They want to find a qualified applicant and use a search firm to confidentially find candidates.
  • An employer is hiring for several positions that will develop and launch a new product line. They don’t want to advertise their openings because it will reveal their plans to their competitors, so they use online tools like LinkedIn to recruit candidates.
  • A small employer needs to replace a long-time employee who is retiring. Instead of posting the job opening, they ask their current employees for referrals.

    • Cost and time reduction.

    • Helps to keep a company's hiring decisions as quiet as possible.

    • Acquires suitable candidates through employee referral. The employees understand the needs of the job and have a vested interest in recommending the good candidate.

    • Provides a bonus option to employees who recommend the applicant who is hired.

Beginning right now, you are cordially invited to enter the hidden job market and stop putting your career in the hands of keyword-searching algorithms and bureaucratic systems.

The #1 reason to aim for a hidden job

Competition for advertised, non-hidden jobs is tougher as more people fight for a smaller number of openings than what are really available.

Put differently- if you can find a relevant hidden job opening, you’ll have much fewer candidates to compete with for the position, if any at all.

Sounds tempting, I know.

How to uncover the hidden job market?
Connect with - in house recruiters, decision makers, external headhunters

  1. Brand yourself for the jobs and/or engagements you want -- not every job you could possibly perform
  2. Make a list of Target Employers/Clients
  3. Research your target firms to find "your" hiring manager in each one -- the person who will have the type of Business Pain you solve, and if the pain is serious enough, also the money to solve it
  4. Reach out to each of your hiring managers with a Pain Letter that talks about pain and solutions, and
  5. Begin to network like crazy -- not in your job-seeker persona but in a consulting persona instead

Introductions - adding value

Jennifer Miftaroska
I learnt from our meeting to tell potential employers what I can do for them right now at the moment and to consider the tense I´m using(better to avoid the past tense). Instead of giving them the standard introduction they might hear from everyone else it´s important to personalize my introduction so that I catch their attention. Previously (or this is also the way I would introduce myself during our meeting) I would have said: My name is Jennifer Miftaroska. I studied Sociology, currently in the final stage of my Masters in Political Science and writing my master thesis on the civil war in Lebanon. 

I now changed it to: I´m a multi-linguistic social scientist and a women´s right advocate with a three years experience in research and teaching. I have a great passion for humanitarian aid work and I attended international trainings throughout the Middle East and Europe. I´m trained in Peace and Security by the OSCE and developed a study for them throughout the 10 month Regional Academy on the UN.  I can help you to conduct thematic research on human rights, gender and security. My teaching experience and background in Sociology enables me to deliver trainings for your employees. Throughout my trainings I created a great network with professionals which might be a great value for your organization.

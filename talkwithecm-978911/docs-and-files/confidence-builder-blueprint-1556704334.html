Confidence Builder Blueprint

fake it til you make it!
-action creates reality
I've known people who spent years trapped behind feeling uncomfortable or shy are amazed that by applying even just one or two of the simple changes I am about to share with you, they notice that people start to treat them differently - they
  • start to take them more seriously
  • and even feel inspired to take action 



 Creating  Confidence - Self confidence with a focus on the value you can add for others
  • Refocus your confidence 
    • Focus on feeling confident about the VALUE you are adding in any given situation. 
    • Everyone can do it - this is where to hang your confidence on.
      • Big things - your skills/experience
      • Small, in the moment, thing - a restaurant tip/knowing where the bathroom is
    • Always be authentic
  • Take these steps before you even start talking
    • Be aware of the setting & audience
    • Face your inner critic 
      • Address your concerns: 
        • Why would anyone listen to me? Your personal experience and viewpoint 
        • What if I don't have the right credentials? I don't have anything new to offer.
      • Remember that people can't see these things until you show them:
        • Your education/experience
        • Your hard work to get where you are today
        • Your kind/funny personality
  • Flex your networking muscles - https://3.basecamp.com/3103412/buckets/978911/documents/1034223006 
    • Be PROACTIVE - Take a few minutes to prepare yourself BEFORE you enter the room
      • Ask yourself - What value can I offer in this specific situation?
      • Develop a strong 10 second introduction
      • Prepare yourself with a few topics
        • Something in the news / tending on social media
        • Something specific to this group of people
        • Avoid controversial topics
    • Set your intention and stay focused on reaching your goal
  • The art of small talk 
    • Use the secret messages behind outward body cues to start conversations
    • Develop simple scripts with the exact words you can use to start a conversation
      • Hi, my name's Elizabeth. What brings you here?

    • Take an active role to help the conversation flow in the direction you want
    • Keep the conversation exciting 
    • Use a clean forward thinking exit
    • Follow up as promised
  • Gamification - Frameworks for approaching people
    • Ask yourself: How do I want this person/people to feel when talking to me? entertained/valued/attracted
    • Eye contact Challenge - Without doing anything out of your normal daily routines, challenge yourself to hold eye contact 1 second longer than you normally do. 
    • 60 second challenge - challenge yourself to introduce yourself to someone (anyone) within 60 seconds of walking into a room.
    • Daily Positive Feedback challenge - challenge yourself to give someone a genuine compliment (something they are wearing, or something they did).

Fake it until you make it - 3 things you can do right now to instantly appear more confident
  1. filler words 
    1. silence vs. filler phrases
    2. chunks & pauses
  2. Experiments
    1. eye contact challenge - one second longer than usual, or ultimate challenge - keep smiling & blinking until the other person looks away :) You won't appear to be a creep, unless you actually are a creep. 
    2. personal space exercises - for a moment get closer, get further, watch what happens! The best part about these experiments is that people tend to react instantly! 
  3. self-judgement - STOP IT! could potentially lead to a downward spiral, focus on staying fresh & light at least in the moment, you should reflect back after the fact, of course! each mistake is a window to the next improvement :)
    1. judging self -make funny faces - micro expressions that can confuse or turn off your listener
    2. judging you - now that we are talking about micro expressions, you might notice them more and more, especially when you are talking with another person, remember most of the time they are about themselves - not you!

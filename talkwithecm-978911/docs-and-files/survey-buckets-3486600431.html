  • I have the fear that as soon as I switch to English people won't take me seriously.
    • You already have a pretty good command of English, maybe you even spent some time abroad polishing your skills, but now you can feel them disappearing. As the old adage says, "Use it or lose it!" You are looking for a way to keep your English skills sharp without working your way through another grammar book. 
  •  It seems that I think of the right things to say only after the opportunity has past. 
    • You feel confident communicating online, where you have time to write out what you want to say (hello google translate!), but you feel like a fish out of water when it comes to LIVE conversations, especially with native speakers. 
  • I feel shy and uncomfortable when I know that I have to speak English. 
    • Although you feel confident in your own language, it seems like everything falls apart as soon as you have to switch to English. You want to speak in a way that is natural for you without feeling forced or stupid.
  • I know that I need to improve my English skills, but I have no idea where to start.
    • You know that English is important and your current English level is limiting your career development.
  • I have tried different English classes, but I'm not getting the results I want.
    • You've learned a lot, you studied, took classes, listen to podcasts, everything, but now you feel stuck.

email scripts!

Here's a list of sample email scripts to help get you started. Use these as launch pads to create your own winning emails.
Copy the information in the emails and edit to your specific situation. I recommend that you add your own personalized touches so that the email sounds both friendly and professional.

The most important thing here is to focus on making the other person feel good and make it easy for them to say yes. No one likes to feel awkward or pressured to help someone they don't even know.


WARM EMAILS -
Writing to people you already know, or at least have had some contact with.


Email:​ Request an introduction from someone you know

Hi (name),
I hope you’re having a great week! 

Last week I drove by our old school and it made me think of you. It’s been awhile since we’ve caught up, let’s plan a coffee date soon!

Your recent post on LinkedIn really sparked my attention. It is so exciting to see how companies and schools are working together to welcome more women into the tech industry.  

Additonally, I wanted to let you know that I’ve been thinking about making a move to the (name your industry) and I noticed that you have some great connections. Would you be comfortable introducing me to (desired contact) at (company)? I’d love to talk to her about her role in the company and her work with (project name). If you wish, I’m happy to write up and send an introduction email to you so that you can simply copy and paste it.

Thank you in advance for your consideration! 
Also, please let me know when you’re available for that coffee date. I’m looking forward to catching up! 


Best, (name) 

How to ask for recommendations for people to talk to

Hello (name),

Hope all is well.

If you recall, we spoke a few months ago at (event) when I was exploring new career opportunities in (industry). Thanks again for (how they helped you).

I was browsing the (company) site the other day and the (job) caught my eye. I think it’d be perfect for me considering my work experience with (example).

From what I remember, it sounds pretty similar to the work you do at (company). By any chance, do you know of anyone there that you think I should talk with? I’d love to learn more about the role so I can see if it’s the right fit for me.

If not, no problem — just wanted to keep you in the loop. Thanks again for all your help!

Take care,
(name)


Follow up email:
I think sending a short email letting them know that you appreciate their contact/help/advice/whatever they gave you & that you sent the application as suggested is a good idea. People generally feel good knowing that someone actually listened & followed their suggestions :)

Something like this: 
Hello (name),
Just wanted to thank you again for talking with me at [name of event]. I sent my CV to (name) as you recommended. I’ll keep you in the loop, and of course, please let me know if there’s anything I can do to repay the favor!
Best,
(name)


COLD EMAILS -
Writing to people you don't know, and don't have any known contacts in common.

Email:​ Request a meeting (from someone you don’t know) 

Hi (name),
I hope you’re having a great week! 

I found your profile on LinkedIn and was impressed with your (background/acomplishment). I’m a (job title) at (company or location) and I’m looking to further develop my career. 

Right now I’m in the research phase of my job search and I was wondering if you’d be available for a quick call to talk about your experience at (company). 

I’m looking forward to hearing from you! 


Best,
(name)

[CONTACT #1] and [CONTACT #2] actually suggested I get in touch. I've been working on (topic) that I think 


How does (date/s) work? I'm available all day, especially the afternoon, and I can meet whenever is convenient for you.


Thanks,


(name)

Alumni contact
To: Jane
From: Samantha
Subject: (school) grad — would love to chat about your work at (company)

Hi (name),

My name is (name). I’m a (university) grad and I came across your name on our alumni site. 

I’d love to get your career advice for 15-20 minutes. I’m currently working at (Company), but many of my friends work in (desired field) and each time they tell me how much they love their job, I get more interested. 

Most of them have told me that if I’m interested in (desired field), I have to talk to someone at (company). Do you think I could ask a few questions about your job and what motivated you to choose (company)? I’d especially love to know how you made your choices after graduating from (university).

I can meet you for coffee, at your office or wherever it’s most convenient for you. 
Would it be possible for us to meet? 

Thanks,

-(name)

“I know you’re probably very busy, but if possible, I’d love to buy you coffee and hear how you got such a leg up over everyone in such a competitive field. I’m really interested in getting into [the field they work in] role at [the type of organization they work at, and I would love to hear your story. If yes, do any of these times work for you: [times] Happy to meet at other times too if something else works better.”

“Fifteen minutes of your time for advice would impact years of my life. I wonder if we could meet”
“I’d love to talk about what has made you successful.

“I have three specific questions on (topic) that I would love to get your insight on over a cup of coffee. Could I get 20 minutes of your time?”

“I have already spoken with numerous friends in the field, but would be particularly interested in any feedback you can provide given you’re accomplishments in ___ and ___.” 

“I would greatly appreciate any pearls of wisdom that you can share!”
“I’m new to this field, and have great respect for the work you have done, particularly in (concrete example) and (second concrete example). I would love the opportunity to meet and gain some insights that may help in my career. Would you be able to spare some time to meet at your office?


“I would love to hear any insight you may have about the industry and your experience at (company). Do you have 15-20 minutes to chat next week, on the phone or over coffee? I really appreciate your consideration.”

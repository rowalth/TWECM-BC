Introduce yourself without feeling awkward

titles:
Introduce yourself without feeling awkward
Build your network without being fake or salesy
Charisma & magnetism made easy
Secrets to Relationships: How to connect with someone you've just met



How to introduce yourself without feeling awkward or completely self-involved?



Do you hate it when people ask you what you do? Not sure how to answer without coming across sale-sy, over the top technical or without twisting your tongue?

I know so many people who struggle to come up with a simple way to introduce themselves.



I used to cringe when I noticed that people didn’t get it and gave me a blank “aha”.


If you don’t have it together you might miss the opportunity to make a great first impression!

We all know that in today’s market it is all about standing out.

Your introduction is one of the most important elements in your personal branding. Whether you want to make a solid first impression in an interview, at a networking event or during a social event, you need to have it together.





How to be approachable:

-dress for confidence! (something that makes YOU feel good & add something unique/shows your personality)
-focus on feeling good/confident/open 
-when you feel comfortable, people a drawn to you (without even knowing why!)
-open body language 
  • eye contact, glance around the room when you enter
  • posture - open arms / or direct everything to what you are interested in
  • relaxed mouth / jaw
-NO ONE likes being rejected! Be kind to each other
-for the non-smilers/resting bitch face - go for relaxed mouth & jaw


When talking:

What to say? =words:
-Start simple - Hi, how are you? I'm Elizabeth..and you are?
-Know a few easy conversation starters, so you are never at a loss for words:
  • What's your story?
  • How / Why questions (ask (adj)interesting questions & people think YOU are (adj) interesting)
  • Tell me more! (magic words!)
  • Mirror technique:
    Repeat to go deeper - If someone mentions something that interests you, repeat it back to ask them for more information. "I went to Croatia last weekend and went camping with some friends." 

    -Question: Oh, do you like camping?  - This mirroring can get people to open up in some very powerful ways
-Statement: Wow - I haven't been camping in so long. It must have been great. - This reveals something about you & shows a connection point, or something that you have in common

Most of these things are great with NEW people, but what about coworkers / family / people you already know...ask about future plans -
Asking about future plans is especially useful with people you already know & see regularly (friends, co-workers, etc.)


It is an open question and always offers new topics.


I'm listening / interested:
  • get closer (slight lean in / step closer) BECAREFUL 
  • hold eye contact with someone you want to meet one second longer
  • react:
    • growing smile
    • lift eyebrows (lightning fast!)
    • slight nod / head tilt (SUPER SUBTLE!!)

These are signals you can send and watch for in others.

These signals come naturally to most people, and I'm not suggesting that you FAKE it, but rather be aware of what you are doing/not doing and what signals you are sending.

A great example of this in action having dental work done...usually people who don't feel comfortable with their smile, for whatever reason, hold back, close their lips / cover their mouth / DON'T smile much. Then once the dental work is complete - boom! They are like a whole new person that just can't stop beaming their pearly whites! Don't believe me, ask any one who's had braces!

before we close, I want to say a few words about leadership:
Introduce yourself to someone

leaning towards someone
arms open
smile / expressive face

A few special notes / add ons:

1. cues of power / leadership:
-smile less
-interrupt (intentionally / unintentionally = vocal power)
-hold eye contact more when speaking / less when listening
-stand still - move only with intention
-hold head still - move only with intention

2. How to answer, "So, tell me, what do you do?"
3 parts:
a) I'm a _______________ ( insert what you do / job title or general description)
-Career change?

For the past several years, I worked as a _______________. But recently, I made

a big shift. These days, I’m doing _______________.

between jobs?

I’m in between “official job titles” right now.

But what I love most in the world is_______________.



b) Who do you help?
 

I work with____________________________

(the person who’s problem you solve. Could be your manager or customers)

Examples:

  • Could be people in a specific position: CEO’s, Executives, Senior Managers, Project manager
  • Departments or companies: Government departments, not for profits, financial institutions etc
  • Could be a combination: Project managers in financial institutions, CFO’s in Accounting firms, Partners in legal companies
  • Could be specific target groups: Mums who are entrepreneurs
c) Which problems do you help solving?

helping them_________________________________

(insert a couple of words describing the big picture of what you do)


d) Which results do you help achieving?

So that they __________________________

(insert concise description of the result the person who’s problem you solve experience).

Try to keep it authentic and conversational – get rid of anything too salesy or that just doesn’t feel like you.

Here are some examples to help you create your own:

I’m a Marketing Strategist and I work with CEO’s helping them create a link between Marketing and Sales so that they can increase engagement with their clients and sell more products.

I’m an IT security specialist and I work with executives in financial institutions helping them ensure the security of the infrastructure and data so that their clients are confident their money is safe.

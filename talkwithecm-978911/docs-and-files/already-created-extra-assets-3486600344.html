1. Conversation Playbook 5 Keys
  • So, you've met, you've started talking and now what? This is a guide for keeping a conversation going and reviving conversations that have taken a wrong turn. This is not about starting or ending conversations - see my How to talk to Anyone guide.
  • #1 - Ask open-ended questions. 
    • So, you are from Chicago. Do you like it there? vs How do you like Chicago? I'm curious to hear more about it!
    • Why? to dig deeper
  • Look for commonalities - things they like to encourage them to open up & talk more
  • Revival questions - here we are looking to save a conversation that has died or completely change the direction of the conversation, and these questions dig for stories
    • Group - so how do you know each other?
    • individual - What's your story? SO OPEN -Most people don't know how to respond to the question, so what do I do for work? Or what do I do for fun? (listen to their tone of voice / expression & you'll probably know which one they prefer to talk about)
    • Asking for advice / tips
  • Ask about future plans - especially good with people you already know & see often, co-workers, etc. it is open, but always new. 
  • Repeat to go deeper - If someone mentions something that interests you, repeat it back to ask them for more information. "I went to Croatia last weekend and went camping with some friends." 
    • With a question: Oh, do you like camping?  - this mirroring can get people to open up in some very powerful ways. 
    • With a statement: because you don't want to ask too many questions and make the person feel interrogated! Wow - I haven't been camping in so long. It must have been great. This reveals something about you & shows a connection point, or something that you have in common. 
2 How to talk to anyone
  • Same for professional - business meetings, networking, colleagues
  • social - parties, new friends, meeting the in-laws for the first time (!)
  • A - set the intention - to guide conversation why are you there? before you even enter the room - what is your game plan?
    • have a good time
    • find more clients
    • learn something 
  • B - Approach - First impressions NOT first words, first LOOK
    • open body language
    • hands out (not in pockets / crossed), shoulders down & back, eye contact / smile
    • #1 - Hi, how are you? I'm Elizabeth. 
    • A genuine compliment - Great presentation! Nice bag...not necessary...
  • B2 - Open for talking
    • check your body position - people talking to each other (head/shoulders/toes - we physically demonstrate our attention - open vs closed)
    • standing around, awkward - check your phone (I've done that!) BUT = vicious cycle (closed body language - head/shoulders down...)
    • get a drink - have something in your hand = something to do, but not as off putting as a cell phone
    • Go to the watering hole - this is the place where animals regularly drink - a meeting space. As humans, we are not so different, 
      • at work, people tend to meet around the water cooler, coffee machine, 
      • at home - kitchen
      • party/ networking event - by the food / bar! They come out (demo- hands full) Where do I go??
    • So, since you just got a drink, stay there & you can be a friendly face, "Hi, How are you? What did you get? Isn't this food great!" 
  • C - Oh! Moments (TK-own word)
    • micro-expression - eyebrow raise, break out of the meh's
    • Examples:
      • meeting the in-laws // looking for things that you have in common, topics to connect...while talking, wait for the OH! moment (Oh! you have a garden!) (even a mini-mini one), then you know that you've hit it! You can follow that topic and go deeper.
      • Clients // sales, talking, making a pitch, presenting common problems & the solutions your product offers - but as your speaking watch for the OH! - you know that you have their interest :)
  • D - Exits - End note / last impression, psychologically speaking, this is really important (lasting impression, the feeling that you leave the person with)
    • Future focus - What are you doing later today / this weekend / next week? Mark a change in the conversation and To shift the thinking of both parties.
    • Thank for conversation / wish the best for future plans
3. Story Map
  • Who: hero/goal/motivation
  • Situation:  where / when
  • Struggle: What is holding you back? Try/fail cycles
  • Crisis: darkest moment
  • Climax: best if self-saving
  • Resolve: return home changed

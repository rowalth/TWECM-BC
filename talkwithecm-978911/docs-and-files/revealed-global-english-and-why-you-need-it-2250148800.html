Revealed: Global English and why you need it

1. English has been called a universal language, right up there with music and mathematics. English is called THE language of business/aviation/technology, the language of opportunity, the world's second language, the list goes on.

According to statistics, It is the world's #1 second language, one of the largest native languages, the official language in 70 countries, and year after year there has been a clear link between a nation's ability to use English and GDP.
Speaking English allows you to actually broaden your world, from job opportunities to the ability to relate to people and expand your reach

2. Global English / international English is the language of SOLVING PROBLEMS

business English  - not just for USA/UK/Australia
Taiwan - Japan = common language = English

Yes, if you want to do business, if you want to take your reach to the next level

examples-
Logistic issues?
Environmental concerns?
Greta Thunberg - who switched from her native Swedish to English to reach her audience - Check out - Fridays for Future

Jay Walker - the name behind Priceline & other initiatives that are changing the way we address old problems: "With English you can become part of a wider conversation -- a global conversation about global problems, like climate change or poverty, or hunger or disease." - AND you can be part of the solutions!

English represents hope for a better future -- a future where the world has a common language to solve common problems.

Yeah, but that's what translators/ interpreters are for, right?

* oh yeah, and one more thing!
So - English is the language for Solving Problems...but what is Global English and how is that different than regular English?

Maybe you've learned English in school, you might even feel pretty confident having a conversation, or perhaps you are even a NATIVE ENGLISH SPEAKER...but then suddenly, something crazy, like a phrasal verb pops up - and everything starts to break down...

A PHRASAL WHAT??

Let's be honest, most native speakers have no idea what a phrasal verb is - even though we use them ALL THE TIME, and for people who speak English as a second or third language...these are those crazy things that you've probably heard about, but can't quite pin down.

(see that? I used a phrasal verb there -to pin down (something or someone) or pin something down -which means to find out some information with certainty / clarity )

To sum up:
Global or international English is all about using English as a means of communication and connecting people and ideas in numerous ways, and the movement towards an international standard for the language.

So, I welcome you to jump onboard and join the conversation!

Now that you have a clearer idea about how to set yourself up for a great introduction, now it is time to put all of this into practice.
But first I want to say a few words about how to talk about yourself. Or more specifically, how to answer that dreaded question, "So, tell me, what do you do?"


simple 4 part recipe to talk about YOU without feeling awkward: 


My Super Savvy Introduction 


My Super Savvy Introduction 
I - solo work - WORKSHEET

1) I'm a _______________ (what you do /job title or general description)
work with some ideas here - deli-lama!

  1. Master of Disaster - helps federal, state, and local authorities access the information they need to recover quickly from calamities - MapInfo Corp.
  2. President and TeaEO - play on words here for the CEO - Honest Tea
special note - in between jobs / career shift! No problem

Career Change? 

In the past, I worked as a (what you did / job title or general description). But recently, I made a big shift.
 Now I'm (what you do / job title or general description)


I'm between jobs right now, but I'm really passionate about 


(desired job title or general description) 





2) and I work with____________________________(who you support /boss, customers) 
this may seem like a simple question, but take some time to consider ALL of the people that you interact with and how you support them / make their lives better


3) helping them_________________________________(the big picture of what you do) 

4) so that they can __________________________(the results you make possible) 
this is extremely important - not only do you want to tell what you do, but this makes it crystal clear to your listener WHY your work is important. 
ok - I help people lose weight...meh, 
better - I help people finally lose the weight they've been carrying around for years so that they can look and feel their best! - do you hear the difference? look and feel their best - I want that! Who doesn't want that? Right?

GOAL: Try to keep it authentic and conversational – get rid of anything too salesy or that just doesn’t feel like you.

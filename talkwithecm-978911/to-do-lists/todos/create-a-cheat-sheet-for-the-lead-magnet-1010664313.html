IDEA #4 - How to win at networking

  • Be first - Don't wait for others to notice you. Start with something simple and be first. 'Hi, my name is Elizabeth. What brings you here?' 
  • Smile - Nearly everyone likes a friendly face. 
  • Make eye contact - Eye contact is the simplest way to connect with others and let them know that you are interested. 
  • Take an active role - Be an active participant in the conversation so that you help the conversation flow in the direction that you want it to go.
  • Dress well - How you look is important for making a good first impression. Most networking events are professional events, and you'd do well to dress the part.
  • Avoid controversial topics - While it is a great idea to talk about news, current events, and a variety of subjects, controversial things should be avoided. Making others uncomfortable is not a great way to make a first impression.
  • Know your goal - Why are you there? Who do you want to meet? Knowing your goal in advance helps keep you motivated and focused on making good connections.
  • Make a clear close - Networking is often about meeting a lot of people in a short amount of time. When it is time to move on, express your thanks and make a clean exit.
